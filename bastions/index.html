What is a Bastion? Overview

A Bastion is a location that belongs to your character — a base, stronghold, or hideout that you build over time. It offers a refuge from adventuring and provides opportunities to craft items, harvest resources, build vehicles, and gain buffs for future quests.

Your Bastion is also a creative space — give your hirelings personalities, design a floor plan, and weave it into your character's story. Up to six players can combine their Bastions to share facilities.

When Can I Start? Build your first basic facility at D-Rank (Level 6). Special facilities unlock at C-Rank (Level 9). To start your Bastion, simply build any basic facility — a Barracks is recommended since it provides defenders.

Building & Costs Core Rules

Construction Rules • You can only build one facility at a time. Don't swap buildings mid-construction.
• Every time you reset your workweek, you also get a Construction Turn. This costs no work hours.
• Facilities become operational at the start of the turn after they complete.
• All Basic Facilities start as Cramped. You cannot skip sizes — Cramped → Roomy → Vast.

Hirelings Core Rules

Many facilities require hirelings to operate. When you finish building a facility that needs one, you get the first hireling for free. Upgrading cramped → vast provides up to two additional hirelings for free on completion.

Moving Hirelings You can move a hireling between facilities of the same rank at any time. To move them into a higher-ranked room, pay the cost difference. Hirelings cannot be lent to other players. You must hire/dismiss hirelings on your Individual Bastion Turn before rolling events.

Acolytes work for free and can staff a room up to one rank above their own. If an event would kill or lose your Acolyte, they're unable to work until the start of your next Individual Bastion Turn.

Bastion Turns Core Rules

Two Types of Turns Monthly Bastion Turn: Happens on the 1st of each real-life month (rolled by a DM). Affects all players at once. A DM may also call for one immediately after a quest or dungeon.

Individual Bastion Turn: Happens when your DM calls for it during a quest. You roll on the Individual Events table. You don't get one every quest.
Orders Once per Individual Bastion Turn, you can issue one order to each of your applicable facilities. Orders let you recruit defenders, harvest materials, craft items, research lore, and more. Each facility's orders are listed in its description below.

Combined Bastions Core Rules

Up to six players can combine their Bastions. Combined players can share and use each other's facilities. However:

  • You can only benefit from the passive bonuses of other players' facilities.
  • When you issue an order, it only affects you and your own bastion, not other players'.
  • Bastion Defenders cannot be lent or given (except via the Hireling transfer rules for one exception).
  • Defenders can be temporarily deployed to help block an attack or lift a siege on an allied bastion.

Defending Your Bastion Combat

You cannot be attacked until you have at least four facilities in your Bastion. Until then, attackers ignore you.

How Bastion Combat Works Each attacker and each Defender rolls their combat die. A Defender dies if they roll 3 or lower on a d6 (but they still kill their attacker). Defenders from Barracks and Graveyards use a d6; Beasts use d6–d12 depending on CR; Knights use d12.

Defenders can only block one attacker at a time, but if they survive they immediately block the next. Keep rolling until all attackers or all Defenders are dead.

Fall of a Bastion Core Rules

CauseWhat Happens
AbandonmentYou voluntarily give it up. Hirelings released; location eventually looted or burned.
NeglectNo orders issued for Monthly Turns equal to your Rank (usually from death/hiatus). Hirelings leave and it's looted. Roughly 6 months of hiatus.
RazedEvery facility is destroyed and you don't repair at least one within 2 monthly turns. All hirelings leave.
TransferenceYour Acolyte can inherit the Bastion if you retire your main and play the Acolyte next. Rooms above the Acolyte's rank are locked until they reach that rank.

Basic Facilities Facilities

Basic facilities are available to all Bastions starting at D-Rank. You can have one of every Basic Facility type. They start Cramped and can be upgraded in size and rank.

Hireling Provision • Cramped = 0 additional hirelings  |  Roomy = 1 hireling  |  Vast = 2 hirelings
• If the facility needs a hireling to operate, you get the first one free when it's completed.

Barracks

Barracks
BasicD-Rank Start

Sleeping quarters for your Bastion Defenders. The first Barracks is a basic facility; any additional Barracks beyond the first takes a special facility slot.

Capacity: 4 Defenders per rank above D, Cramped. Roomy adds 2 per rank; Vast adds 4 per rank.

Order Recruit: Recruit up to 4 Bastion Defenders and assign them to the Barracks. Free of gold cost.

Bedroom

Bedroom
BasicD-Rank Start

Where you and your hirelings sleep. Long resting in your Bastion grants you 1d4 + Con Modifier Max HP. Add 1d4 per rank above D.

Roomy: Share this benefit with up to 4 others in your combined Bastion during the same turn.
Vast: Share with up to 9 others, even those outside your combined Bastion.

A player can only gain Max HP equal to their rank — not the room's rank.

Battlements

Battlements
BasicMust match your Rank

Walls and archers. Must be the same rank as you to function.

Cramped: Repel Attackers — sacrifice Battlements to push enemies back when all Defenders fall.
Roomy: Reduce Defender death threshold by 1 (die on 2 or lower instead of 3).
Vast: Reduce by another 1 (die on 1 only). Applies to all Defenders including knights, undead, and beasts.

Order Mount Defenses: Activate Repel Attackers and Reduce Defender Death benefits.

Order Rebuild: 500g per rank above C (D-Rank is free). Issue 3 times to fully rebuild. Pay 5× cost to rebuild by your next Individual Turn instead.

Dining Room

Serves 1 (Cramped), 4 (Roomy), 9 (Vast).

Well Room

Well Room
Basic

Reliable drinking water for your entire Bastion. Each size of your Well Room lets one additional special facility remain operational during a siege.

Order Fresh Water: Keep facilities running during a siege. Vast Well Room extends this benefit to your Combined Bastion.

Harvestarium

Harvestarium
BasicHireling: 1 (Cramped), 3 (Roomy)

Choose a specialization when building (Fertile Soil, Quarries, Mines, Hunter's Lodge, Pasturelands, or Black Forest). Boosts your economy by letting you harvest extra materials when you earn Rewards.

Order Harvest: When you gain a Reward, roll 1d4–1 (min 1) on a specialization table for extra materials at 3 ranks below the facility's rank. Once per Individual Bastion Turn.

Vast Harvestarium: Choose one extra specialization.

Humble Exterior

Humble Exterior
BasicMust match your Rank

Requirements: Must NOT have an Armoury, Battlements, Siege Workshop, or War Room constructed, and no more than one Barracks.

Your Bastion looks unimportant. Both the Individual "Surprise Attack!" event and the Monthly "Siege" event are treated as "Nothing Happens" for you.

If you have a Vast Bedroom, you can house 5 reserve hirelings not tied to any specific facility, letting you instantly fill vacancies caused by events.

Special Facilities Facilities

Special facilities are highly specialized rooms available from C-Rank onward. You are limited by the number of slots you have.

Key Rules • Each facility has a minimum rank requirement and sometimes a prerequisite (feat, spell, etc.).
• Rank upgrades cost the same as Basic Facilities (500g → C, 1,000g → B, etc.). No extra build time.
• Cannot skip ranks — you must upgrade C → B → A → S in order.
• You can destroy a special facility instantly at no cost to free the slot for something new.

Spending an entire Long Rest in your Observatory gives you a magical Charm (lasts 1 Bastion Turn or until used). The Charm lets you cast Contact Other Plane without a spell slot. Cannot gain another while you still have it.

Order Augury: Cast Augury once per day as an action.

S-Rank — Order: Supernatural Gift An unknown power grants you or someone you name on the same plane one of: Charm of Darkvision, Charm of Heroism, or Charm of Vitality (from DMG). Expires after a long rest if unused.
Pub
Rank ARoomy1 Hireling
Prerequisite: Brewer's Supplies proficiency

Your Pub has a special "Pub Special" beverage. Each pub special produces 2 consumables treated as Very Rare potions.

Order Change Keg: Swap to a new pub special and restock.

Order Restock: Restock all current pub specials.

Sample Specials: Bigby's Burden (Enlarge 24 hrs) · Kiss of the Spider Queen (Spider Climb 24 hrs) · Moonlight Serenade (Darkvision 60ft 24 hrs) · Positive Reinforcement (Necrotic Resistance 24 hrs) · Sterner Stuff (Immune to Frightened 24 hrs) · Scurvy's Surprise (Wild Magic effect) · and more.

Pub of Legend (2,000g) Increases to 4 hirelings. With 2+ hirelings, add a second pub special on tap. S-Rank: a third pub special.
Reliquary
Rank ARoomy1 Hireling
Prerequisite: Ability to use a Holy Symbol or Druidic Focus

Order Reliquary Charm: Gain a Charm letting you cast Greater Restoration once without a spell slot or material components. Cannot regain while you still have it.

Order Arcane Talisman: Commission a talisman (amulet, box, or statuette) ready at your next Bastion Turn. Use it in place of one spell's material components up to 125g. It becomes inert until you use this order again. Cannot split the cost. S-Rank: covers up to 250g.

Bones of the Arcane Martyr (2,000g) Talisman covers material components up to 250g. At S-Rank + Arcane Martyr: covers up to 750g.

Rank S — Special Facilities

Demiplane
Rank SVast1 Hireling
Prerequisite: Ability to use an Arcane Focus as a Spellcasting Focus

A 5×10 ft door appears on any flat surface in your Bastion. If in your Bastion during a turn, you can relocate the door. Only you and your hirelings can open it. Leads to a stone-room Demiplane that is its own plane of existence. Neither the Demiplane nor its door can be dispelled.

Order Arcane Resilience: Magical runes appear on the Demiplane's walls for 1 Bastion Turn. After a full Long Rest inside, gain Temp HP = 5× your level.

Fabrication: In the Demiplane, use a Magic action to conjure a nonmagical object (max 5-ft cube, max 5g value, wood/stone/clay/glass/paper/non-precious crystal/metal). Once per Long Rest.

The Demiplane spell can connect to any Bastion with this facility if you've visited that Demiplane before.

Guildhall
Rank SVast1 Hireling
Prerequisite: Workshop or Laboratory (shares that facility's slot)

Built into the same slot as one of your Vast Workshops or Vast Laboratories. You become the guild master of a guild of ~50 skilled members in nearby settlements.

Order Guild Assignment: Commission guild members to perform a special assignment. See the Guilds section for what each guild can do.

Sanctum
Rank SRoomy4 Hirelings
Prerequisite: Ability to use a Holy Symbol or Druidic Focus

Sanctum Charm: After a Long Rest in your Bastion, gain a Charm to cast Heal once without a spell slot. Cannot regain while you still have it.

Order Fortifying Rites: Inspire hirelings to perform daily rites benefiting you or one named character. Each time the beneficiary finishes a Long Rest, they gain Temp HP = your level. Lasts one Bastion Turn.

Sanctum Recall: While the Sanctum exists, Word of Recall is always prepared and can target your Sanctum. One creature who arrives via this spell also receives a Heal spell.

War Room
Rank SVast2+ Lieutenants
Prerequisite: A Title recognised by people

Comes with 2 battle-hardened Lieutenant hirelings (Veterans, aligned with you). Each Lieutenant housed in your Bastion reduces Defender casualties from attacks by 1. Can have up to 10 Lieutenants.

Order Recruit Lieutenants: Gain 1 new Lieutenant.

Order Recruit Soldiers: Each Lieutenant musters up to 100 Guards (or 20 mounted on Riding Horses). Cost: 3g per Guard and Horse at recruitment and each Bastion Turn thereafter. Army must be led by you or a Lieutenant. Disbands if you refuse to pay or command it.

Order Hold the Line! During an event, each Guard contributes 1d6 (1d12 if mounted). Lieutenants lead but don't fight.

War Room can also be used to defend against Surprise Attacks.
