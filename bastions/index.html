Your stronghold, your legacy — build it, defend it, grow it.

Quick Start New Players

  1. Reach D-Rank / Level 6.
  2. Build your first Basic Facility. Once it is complete, your Bastion officially exists.
  3. You gain one Construction Turn every workweek reset. Construction does not use workweek hours.
  4. Build and upgrade Basic Facilities over time. Basic Facilities start Cramped and must upgrade step by step.
  5. Reach C-Rank / Level 9 to unlock Special Facilities.
  6. Issue Bastion Orders during Bastion Turns, defend your Bastion from events, and grow your stronghold into a lasting legacy.

What is a Bastion? Overview

A Bastion is a location that belongs to your character: a base, stronghold, ship, hideout, temple, tower, keep, casino, lodge, or other seat of power. It offers refuge from adventuring and gives access to crafting bonuses, harvesting, defenders, hirelings, buffs, and story events.

A Bastion is also a creative playground. It gives your character a storytelling space where pets, NPC friends, servants, hirelings, allies, and personal projects can have a home and become part of the world.

When Can I Start?Build your first Basic Facility at D-Rank / Level 6. Special Facilities begin at C-Rank / Level 9.

Building & Costs Construction

Starting Your BastionTo start your Bastion, build any Basic Facility. Once that first facility is completed, your Bastion officially exists. All Basic Facilities begin as Cramped and must be upgraded step by step. You cannot skip directly from Cramped to Vast.
Construction TurnsEvery time you get a workweek reset, you also get a Construction Turn. A Construction Turn does not take any workweek hours. You can only build one facility at a time unless a rule specifically says otherwise. Facilities become operational at the start of the turn after completion.

Hirelings Core Rules

If the basic or special facility you just built needs a hireling, it provides the first one for free. If you upgrade a facility from Roomy to Vast, you get up to two additional hirelings for free upon completion. After that, new hirelings must be purchased.

Facility RankHireling Cost

C200g
B300g
A400g
S500g

This is a one-time expense that covers salary and upkeep going forward. You must pay this cost even if the hireling is an NPC invited to your Bastion during or after an adventure.

Cost Reductions

  • Charisma: 13+ = –25%, 15+ = –50%, 17+ = –75%, 19+ = free. Magic items count.
  • Disguise Kit: Proficient = –10%; Expertise = –25%.
  • Costs cannot be reduced below 0.
  • Combined Bastion: Share half your reduction with allies. Only the highest shared reduction applies.

Moving Hirelings

You can move a hireling from any basic or special facility to another within your Bastion as long as the facilities are the same rank. If moving a hireling into a higher-ranked room, pay the cost difference. If your total reduction is 100% or more, moving them is free.

  1. At the start of your Bastion Turn, hire new hirelings, move existing hirelings, or upgrade them.
  2. Roll your Individual Bastion Turn event.
  3. After rolling, you cannot hire new hirelings until your next Bastion Turn.
  4. You may still move or upgrade existing hirelings until the beginning of your next Bastion Turn.

Hirelings cannot be lent. If a non-Acolyte hireling is given to another player for roleplay purposes, that player treats the hireling as a new acquisition and pays the normal cost each time the hireling changes hands.

Acolytes as Hirelings

If you have an Acolyte, they can work as a hireling as long as they meet the facility prerequisites. Acolytes work for free and can work in a room up to one rank above their own. If an event would kill or lose your Acolyte, they cannot work in any facility until the beginning of your next Individual Bastion Turn.

Bastion Turns Core Rules

Bastion Turn SummaryMonthly Bastion Turns happen on the first day of each real-life month and affect all players. Individual Bastion Turns happen when a DM calls for one during or after a quest. Construction Turns happen every workweek reset and are used to build or upgrade facilities.

There are two kinds of Bastion Turns: Monthly Bastion Turns and Individual Bastion Turns.

  • Monthly Bastion Turn: Happens on the first day of every real-life month and is rolled by a Dungeon Master.
  • Individual Bastion Turn: Happens when a DM calls for it during or immediately after a quest or dungeon. You do not get one every quest unless the DM calls for it.
Bastion OrdersOnce per Individual Bastion Turn, you can issue one order to each one of your facilities if applicable.

Combined Bastions Core Rules

  • Up to six players can combine their Bastions.
  • Players in a Combined Bastion can share all facilities.
  • You can only benefit from passive bonuses of someone else's facilities.
  • When you initiate a Bastion Order, it only affects you and your Bastion, not other players.
  • Bastion hirelings and defenders cannot be lent or given, except for the roleplay exception under Moving Hirelings.
  • Bastion Defenders can be temporarily deployed to block an attack or lift a siege.

Defending Your Bastion Combat

You cannot be attacked until you have at least four facilities in your Bastion. If an attack event happens before then, the enemy ignores you.

Barracks, Graveyards, and Menageries let you hire or house Defenders. Some events can grant extra Defenders, such as Mercenary Guest, Ronin, or Duel to the Death. Hiring Defenders from a Barracks does not cost gold, but other events and facilities might.

Bastion combat works by rolling a Defender's combat die. If the roll is 3 or lower, the Defender dies but successfully kills their adversary. This can be improved by Battlements or an Armory. Barracks and Graveyard Defenders normally use a d6, beasts may range from d6 to d12, and Knights use d12.

Event Combat NoteUnless otherwise stated, any event that requires a Defender to roll a combat die uses the normal Bastion combat rules. A Defender that rolls within their death threshold is killed. Improvements from Battlements, Armory, or other Bastion features apply if they would normally apply.

You may sacrifice Battlements to repel an attack, or release a Friendly Monster if you have one. Repelling an attack by destroying Battlements or a similar defense grants no reward.

Siege weapons block a set number of attackers or deal damage during an Invasion. Ballistas kill 6 attackers or deal 3d10. Catapults kill 8 attackers or deal 10d6. Cannons kill 16 attackers or deal 8d10.

Golden Age

All gold rewards from quests and dungeons increase by 10%. You also roll twice on all Reward tables in this book and choose either result. This does not apply to Harvestarium rewards.

If you have a Workshop, you gain Expertise when crafting with the associated proficiency until the beginning of the next Monthly Turn.

Drought

Water becomes scarce and hirelings are exhausted. All facilities must have their maximum number of hirelings to operate. Hireling costs are doubled and your Barracks can recruit only 2 Defenders at a time instead of 4 until the next Monthly Turn.

If you have a Vast Well Room, ignore this event.

Storm

A tornado or severe storm strikes your Bastion. One facility, basic or special, is chosen at random and destroyed. All hirelings inside that facility die. A destroyed facility can be rebuilt at half total cost and 3 Construction Turns.

Festival

If you have a Pub, Reliquary, Sanctuary, Gaming Hall, Sanctum, or Theatre, the party comes to your Bastion and you receive a gift from a visiting noble. Roll once on the Reward table for your rank with advantage, even if you have more than one qualifying facility.

If you do not have one of those facilities, your hirelings leave to enjoy the festival until your next Individual Turn. One hireling for each rank you are above D does not return; you choose who is lost.

Refugees

A group of refugees arrives seeking protection and work. All hireling costs are reduced by 50%, stacking with other reductions such as high Charisma. Barracks can recruit 6 Defenders at a time instead of 4 until the next Monthly Turn.

Siege

Your Bastion is surrounded by a hostile force. Facilities that require at least one hireling stop functioning while under siege. You still gain Construction Turns. The sieging force does not attack, even if you attack them.

Well Rooms and Battlements allow some facilities to continue functioning. You can end the siege early by defeating the besiegers over one or more Individual Bastion Turns. Siege weapons and War Room can be used. A Friendly Monster can repel the siege and then departs.

Invasion

A hostile kingdom marches toward Drathmere. On the 15th day of the month, all players may send Defenders to withstand the assault. One player’s Bastion is chosen as the war site, and facilities that normally affect only that player’s Defenders now affect everyone until the war ends.

Once the event begins counting on the 15th, no more forces can be sent. Each player rolls the combat dice for all sent Defenders and totals the results. A Friendly Monster fights with 10d12 instead of repelling the event. Siege weapons may be used.

  • 10,000+: The Invasion is crushed. All participating players roll twice on the Reward table.
  • 5,000+: The Invasion is repelled. Half of each participating player’s Defenders are killed, chosen by that player. Roll once on the Reward table.
  • Below 5,000: Drathmere is defeated. All sent Bastion Defenders are killed and each participating player’s Battlements are destroyed.
Search Party

A VIP has disappeared and the guild forms a search party. The search can happen any time during the month. Players send Defenders, roll each Defender’s combat die, total all results, and submit the total to a DM.

You cannot send siege weapons, but you can use War Room. If the combined total is 1,000 or more, the VIP is found. Everyone who sent at least one Defender rolls on the Reward table for their rank. If one player contributed 50% or more of the total, that player rolls twice.

Abnormal Phenomenon

Hold your breath. The DMs are whispering. This event is intentionally strange, unusual, and determined by the DM team.

Individual Event Details Expanded

Plague

A random hireling is on the brink of death. There is no time to ask other players for help. Pass a DC 15 Medicine check or feed them an Elixir of Health. If saved, they rest until your next Bastion Turn. If not saved, they die.

Animal Rescue

A hireling finds a dying wild animal. Save it with a healing spell or a DC 15 Medicine check. If saved, it becomes an extra Defender until the end of the next attack, at which point it dies or returns to the wild. A DC 18 Animal Handling check convinces it to stay until death.

Wandering Professionals

Visitors arrive and one asks to become a hireling for free.

If you defeat an attacking force larger than your total Defenders, roll once on the Reward table. Repelling by sacrificing Battlements or similar defenses grants no reward.

Criminal Hireling

A hireling is discovered to have a criminal past. You may pay a bribe of 1d6 × 10 GP per rank to keep them, or allow the arrest. If arrested, the hireling is lost permanently.

Duel to the Death

A wandering knight arrives, boasting he is the greatest swordsman in Thracia. He challenges your strongest Defender to a duel.

Choose one Defender as your champion. Siege weapons and Friendly Monsters cannot participate. Roll your champion’s combat die three times. Normal Defenders use 1d6, Knights use 1d12, and other Defenders use their normal combat die.

Your champion follows normal Bastion combat rules and dies if they roll within their death range. For example, a normal Defender dies on a roll of 3 or lower. A Defender protected by Battlements or Armory improvements may have a lower death threshold.

If your champion dies during any of the three rolls, you lose and the knight departs. If your champion survives all three rolls, you win and the knight joins your Bastion as a Knight Defender using 1d12. The knight takes care of their own accommodations.

Extraordinary Opportunity

Your Bastion may host a festival, fund a renowned spellcaster, or appease a noble. Roll 3d6. If all three dice match, roll again on the Individual Bastion Events table or take an Art Object worth 250g.

Guest

A friendly guest arrives. Roll 1d4.

  1. Renowned Individual: Stays 7 days and gives a letter of recommendation.
  2. Seeking Sanctuary: Stays 7 days while evading persecution and gives a 250g Art Object as thanks.
  3. Mercenary Guest: Stays until sent away or killed and provides one extra Bastion Defender without housing.
  4. Friendly Monster: A creature such as a brass dragon or treant stays. If your Bastion is attacked or sieged, it repels the threat, preventing Defender loss, then departs.
Tragic Accident

One random facility suffers an accident. All hirelings in it die. The facility closes until your next Bastion Turn unless you pay 200g to repair it immediately.

Request for Aid

A local leader asks for help. You can send up to 4 Defenders and/or hirelings per rank above D. Roll 1d6 for each participant sent. If the total meets or beats 12 per rank above D, the problem is solved and you roll on the Reward table.

If the total is lower, the problem remains unresolved and you gain nothing. Siege weapons cannot be sent, but War Room can be used.

Ronin

A warrior offers service as a Knight for 250g, or for free if you beat him at Bandit’s Pig. Roll 3d6 six times. If any roll shows three 6s, you win because they look like pig tails; the Ronin joins for free and you gain a Reward. If any roll shows three 1s, you lose, but may still hire him for 250g.

The Ronin joins your defenders as a Knight using 1d12.

Sellswords

Eight defenders arrive. You may take as many as your Barracks can hold. If you lack space, the rest are turned away. Alternatively, two of them may become free hirelings.

Treasure

Vermin Infestation

All kitchens and laboratories shut down until the vermin are exterminated. Pay 1d6 × 100 GP or send ten Defenders into the cellars.

Roll 1d6 for defenders, undead, and knights, and 2d6 for beasts, including CR 0 beasts. If the total exceeds 40, you succeed. If you fail, you must pay 50g for the peasants to finish the job.

Love Letter

You receive an anonymous love letter. The details are roleplay-focused and may be developed by the player and DM.

Two Hirelings Get Married

Two of your hirelings get married. This is primarily a roleplay event unless a DM adds further complications or rewards.

Shop

Hireling Capacity: 1 Roomy, 3 Vast.

A Shop is a dedicated business space for selling trade goods and items in the Discord Server's Market channel. Without this, you cannot start one.

Storage

Hireling Capacity: 1 Cramped, 3 Roomy.

Storage allows you to store equipment while adventuring. Cramped stores 2 categories, Roomy stores 5, and Vast stores all categories.

OrderStore: Store and withdraw goods or equipment any number of times.

Well Room

A reliable water source for defenders, hirelings, and crafters.

OrderFresh Water: Each size increases the number of Special Facilities that remain operational during a siege, up to 3. A Vast Well Room extends these benefits to your Combined Bastion.

Battlements

Battlements must be equal to your rank to function.

OrderMount Defenses: Repel attackers and reduce Defender deaths.

Repel Attackers: If all Defenders are killed, sacrifice Battlements to push attackers back. Destroyed walls must be rebuilt.

Reduce Defender Death: Defenders normally die on 3 or lower. This reduces the threshold by 1; Vast Battlements can make defenders die only on a 1.

Harvestarium

Hireling Capacity: 1 Cramped, 3 Roomy.

Choose a specialization when building: Fertile Soil, Quarries, Mines, Hunter's Lodge, Pasturelands, or Black Forest.

OrderHarvest: When you gain a Reward, roll once on a specialization table for 1d4–1, minimum 1, materials at a rank three lower than the facility.

Humble Exterior

Requirements: No Armory, Battlements, Siege Workshop, or War Room, and no more than one Barracks.

You treat the Individual Event Surprise Attack! and Monthly Event Siege as Nothing Happens.
